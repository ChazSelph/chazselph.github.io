Johan Willem Frederik Werumeus Buning was born in Velp, Netherlands on May 4, 1891. The portrait below depicts him in 1912 at around 21 years old, before his literary journey began. He studied to become a lawyer and public official, but quit to become art editor for De Telegraaf. He was encouraged to write poetry by his friend, the "Prince of Dutch Poets," Adriaan Roland Holst. In 1921, he published his first collection of poems- In memoriam-, which dealt with the death of his lover. He followed up with Hemel en aarde in 1927. Other major works include Mária Lécina, een lied in honderd verzen (1932).

He was in his mid-fifties when he experienced a one-year publication ban on his work. It was a retaliatory measure for having joined the Nederlandsche Kultuurkamer during the German occupation in WW2. The Kultuurkamer was an institute established by the Germans to which all artists, architects, writers, journalists, musicians, film actors and stage performers had to be affiliated in order to work. Although he soon returned to work as a journalist at Elseviers Weekblad, his reputation was permanently damaged and his popularity declined. This fate parallels that of fellow writer Knut Hamsun in Norway.

He died at 67 years old on November 16, 1958 in Amsterdam.

A Poem written by J.W.F. Werumeus Buning and translated by Chaz Selph.

Dutch English

Een oud vers

An old verse
Wat ik betreur te hebben niet bezeten What I regret is not having possessed
Is het geluk van menig burgerman: the good fortune of many a commoner:
De vrede van het huisgezin, en van The peace of a family home, and of
De kinderen, die mee aan tafel eten. children who eat alongside me at the table.
En ik weet wel, dat in mijn arm gelegen And I do know, that my darling
De liefste is bezwijmd van zaligheid, has lain in my arm, swooned from bliss,
Dat ik de stem ken van de eeuwigheid, I know of Eternity's voice,
En van het hart, dat mijn hart is genegen. And of the heart to whom mine is devoted.
Maar dit is alles niets, al deze dingen, But all of this is nothing, all of these things,
Gezegend, en te min; 't is eens niet meer; Blessed, and not good enough; it does not last;
Men hoort de vogels in de bomen zingen, One hears the birds singing in the trees,
De jaren gaan, de winter keert steeds weer, Years go by, the unrelenting winter returns,
De sterren staan. Ik heb niet goed gekozen. The stars stand. I have not chosen wisely.
Wat doet een bedelaar met rode rozen? What does a beggar do with red roses?
